Colombia Lacrosse Board Member

Running a non-profit national team is a complex yet deeply rewarding endeavor that blends the intensity of high-level sports with the mission-driven heart of service. At its core, it requires a passionate commitment not only to the athletes, but also to the broader community the organization represents. Unlike commercial sports teams, a non-profit national team often operates on a shoestring budget, relying on donations, sponsorships, grants, and volunteer support to fund travel, uniforms, training, facilities, and administrative costs. Every dollar must be accounted for and directed toward maximum impact. Leaders juggle roles as coaches, fundraisers, marketers, and mentors—wearing many hats with limited resources. Logistics can be daunting, especially when coordinating international travel, organizing competitions, or meeting federation requirements with minimal staff. Balancing competitive goals with accessibility and inclusivity adds further complexity. Yet, this is also where the mission shines through. Running such a team creates opportunities for athletes who may not have access to elite-level programs otherwise. It empowers youth, builds national pride, and can even promote social change, especially when the team represents underrepresented or emerging communities. Success is measured not just in medals but in stories of resilience, growth, and teamwork. The day-to-day involves managing tight schedules, negotiating with vendors, advocating with governing bodies, and ensuring athletes have the physical, mental, and emotional support they need. Often, the people involved are driven by pure passion—volunteers who give their time because they believe in the bigger picture. The rewards are rarely financial, but they are deeply meaningful. Seeing athletes compete on a global stage with a flag on their chest and a community behind them is a powerful experience. It’s not easy work—but it’s mission-driven, people-powered, and capable of leaving a legacy that far outlasts any single season.

Competing on a national team is both exhilarating and deeply meaningful. Every game is a chance to represent something bigger than yourself—your country, your community, and your teammates. The intensity of competition brings out the best in you, pushing your limits and sharpening your skills. There's nothing quite like the adrenaline of stepping onto the field, the camaraderie of shared goals, and the pride of wearing your nation’s colors. Beyond the challenge, it’s genuinely fun—traveling with friends, experiencing new cultures, and creating lifelong memories. Win or lose, the journey itself is unforgettable and truly special.

Chicago Portagers Sr.A Box Lacrosse

Senior A (Sr. A) box lacrosse in North America represents the highest level of elite, fast-paced indoor lacrosse, combining skill, toughness, and deep tradition. Teams compete in front of passionate fans, showcasing the best talent outside the National Lacrosse League. The Chicago Portagers are an exciting addition to this scene, bringing high-level box lacrosse to the Midwest. Representing grit, pride, and opportunity, the Portagers are building a strong identity rooted in Chicago’s rich sports culture. They provide a platform for top-tier athletes to compete, grow the game, and inspire a new generation of fans and players across the region.

Evanston Youth Lacrosse Association

Coaching the U-12/U-10 age group is one of the most rewarding and influential roles in youth sports. At this stage, players are developing foundational skills, growing their understanding of the game, and forming lasting attitudes toward teamwork and competition. As a coach, it’s about balancing fun with structure—creating an environment where kids can build confidence, try new things, and learn from mistakes. It's also a time to teach values like sportsmanship, effort, and respect. The bonds formed with players and families are incredibly meaningful, and seeing their progress both on and off the field makes every practice worth it.
